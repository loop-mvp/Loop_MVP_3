Scene 1

Customer signs up and chooses a guided onboarding path

Loop starts by asking what the customer is launching, who it is for, and whether they want to build from scratch or import existing materials.

Guided Start

Signup Flow

1
Create account
Founder or PMM joins Loop and creates a workspace.
2
Select workflow
New product launch, messaging refresh, or repositioning.
3
Invite team
Add co-founder, PMM, marketer, or sales lead.

Onboarding Prompts

  • What product are you launching?
  • Who is the primary customer?
  • What problem does this product solve?
  • Do you already have messaging, docs, or a pitch deck to import?
  • What is your target launch date?

What Loop does here

It reduces blank-page anxiety with a guided setup instead of dropping the user into a huge strategy canvas immediately.

Scene 2

They build product truth one section at a time

The customer fills out problem, solution, audience, capabilities, competitors, and differentiation using guided prompts and smaller canvases.

Product Truth

Problem Statement

Core problem, stakes, and why current alternatives fall short.

Solution

What the product does, how it solves the problem, and why now.

Audience

Primary buyer, influencer, pains, jobs to be done, and buying triggers.

Capabilities

Feature-to-benefit mapping helps founders turn product details into customer value language.

Competition

Loop captures alternatives, win/loss context, and differentiators to sharpen downstream messaging.

Best workflow

Start with Product Truth because it gives every AI suggestion, narrative draft, and launch asset a stronger factual base.

Scene 3

Loop turns raw product truth into an approved narrative

Once the product inputs are clear, the customer moves into Core Narrative to define positioning, value proposition, messaging pillars, headline, and pitch.

Core Narrative

Positioning Statement

Loop guides founders into a sharper market-facing statement, not just a feature list.

Value Proposition

The core promise gets distilled into business value and customer outcomes.

Messaging Pillars

Three to four repeatable messages become the narrative spine for launch and sales.

AI Assistant Support

Loop suggests stronger messaging, flags generic phrasing, and recommends additions like objection handling, proof points, and launch messaging packs.

How the AI helps

Not as a generic chatbot first, but as a section-aware assistant that improves clarity, differentiation, and consistency.

Scene 4

They move into GTM and create launch-ready assets

With the narrative approved, Loop helps the team operationalize it across channels, launch plans, and asset generation.

GTM + Assets

GTM

Define launch goal, segment focus, channels, milestones, and what sales and marketing need to say.

Assets Generated

  • Website headline and hero copy
  • Launch email draft
  • Sales one-pager
  • Battlecard
  • Product summary sheet

Why this matters

Loop is no longer just a strategy workspace. It becomes a bridge from product truth to launch execution.

Monetizable moment

This is where the product starts feeling sellable: teams use one approved narrative to produce real launch outputs faster.

Scene 5

Loop gives review feedback before launch goes live

The assistant flags gaps in proof, weak differentiation, and inconsistent claims. Teams review and resolve these before publishing.

Review Cycle

Flag

Positioning is too generic for the category.

Flag

Messaging claims lack proof points or metrics.

Flag

Sales and website copy drift from the approved narrative.

Review Workflow

Owners resolve items, approvers sign off, and Loop stores what changed before the team publishes a new narrative version.

Why people keep using it

Because Loop helps catch narrative drift and launch risk before assets go live, not after.

Scene 6

The team launches with one aligned story

Once the review items are closed and the narrative is approved, the launch goes live with assets, sales messaging, and positioning aligned.

Launch Live

Published Outputs

Approved launch narrative, live website copy, GTM brief, outbound messaging, and internal enablement docs.

Team Outcome

Founders, PMM, marketing, and sales all work from the same source of truth instead of fragmented docs and prompts.

Loop's role

It acts as the narrative operating system from input through execution, not just a place to write.

Value to the customer

Fewer review loops, faster launch readiness, and stronger cross-functional alignment.

Scene 7

Loop tracks feedback, analytics, and narrative health after launch

The customer sees how the message is landing and where the story may need refinement for the next iteration.

Feedback + Analytics

Signals

Website engagement, win/loss notes, sales objections, customer feedback.

Narrative Health

Loop scores clarity, consistency, proof, and launch readiness across versions.

Recommendations

Update positioning, tighten messaging pillar two, add stronger proof for competitive claims.

Feedback loop

Launch does not end the workflow. Loop feeds real-world learning back into Product Truth and Narrative for the next cycle.

Retention engine

Analytics and feedback are what keep Loop useful after the initial launch project is over.

From signup to live launch, Loop becomes the system of record for product narrative
